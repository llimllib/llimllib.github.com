No Development Today

I've been playing with my new mp3 player too long, so no development news today. The player, an iRiver ihp-120, is excellent so far. I wanted it over an iPod because it simply acts as an external hard drive, with no acrobatics. It works on every platform; although I haven't tried it on my Linux partition yet, I've read that usb_hotplug detects it just like any other portable hd.

The only other thing I've been doing is uploading my music profile to a recommendations/community music site, and I'm very impressed with it. The site is called Audioscrobbler, and it's related closely to last.fm. To use them both (at the same time) just download a winamp (or iTunes or xmms or...) plugin to your computer and play music like normal. In the background, it uploads to their servers what songs you've been playing, and builds your profile from that information.

You can see what I've been listening to at my profile page at last.fm, and even listen to a radio station consisting of random songs from my playlist.

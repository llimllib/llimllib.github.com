Math

Latent Semantic Indexing is a technique for searching a body of documents for a series of terms. The user of LSI creates a term-document matrix, performs SVD on it, and comes up with a new term-document matrix (hopefully more useful) with which to search.

However, the technique can be twisted to create a matrix of term-term similarity values. In this matrix, the value of the (m, n) cell may be interpreted as the similarity of term m to term n.

I am trying to use this term-term matrix to determine what artists are similar to what other artists. Audioscrobbler, which I've mentioned before, has produced a large body of data showing relating users to the artists they listen to. Theoretically, if user A listens to band 1 and band 2, and user B listens to band 2 and band 3, then band 1 and band 3 are probably related.

I want to test whether LSI term-term matrices can reveal this information in an interesting way. Supposedly, they can, and this provides an interesting subjective training ground for the algorithm.

I've coded up an LSI module in Python, as well as used SVDLIBC through Python, as I begin to test my hypothesis. Hopefully, soon, I should have pretty graphs and charts to put up here, but I'm not yet confident in my results.
